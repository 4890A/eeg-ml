Intro

Applications of Machine Learning with Biometrics

Electroencephalography (EEG) is a method which measures the electrical activity of the brain using electrodes. Clinically, EEGs are most often used in the diagnosis of epilepsy, sleep disorders, coma, encephalopathies, and depth of anesthesia. With the rapid advancement of machine learning in the last few decades, researchers have been able to train algorithms to interpret and classify complex EEG signals. The ability to interpret these signals has opened up many possibilities for the application of EEG such as the development of mind-controlled prosthetics for amputees, robotic exoskeletons for motor impaired individuals to even the recreation of dreams.

As a demonstration of some of the ways that we can apply machine learning to biometrics, we trained various neural networks on open source data and compare their capacity to interpret and classify the data. We also attempted to gather raw data via a commercially available EEG, the Muse headband and train a neural network on simple activities.

Classifying Motion



About Phone Watch


Smart phones and watches use gyroscopes and accelerometers to detect motion. We analyzed the HHAR dataset, collected from phones and watches worn by people engaged in activities such as walking, biking, and ascending stairs to see how well the data from those devices predicts the activities. A basic neural network was used first, which predicted activity with an approximate accuracy of 65%, and final obtained a better predictive accuracy of 87% using a Long Short Term Memory (LSTM) network, which is a type of Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) which considers not just the current input, but what it remembers about the preceding elements.
